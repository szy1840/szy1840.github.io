There's a particular kind of clarity that only arrives after you've tried to explain a thing to someone else, or, failing that, to a blank page. For me, building software and writing prose turned out to be the same activity wearing different clothes. Both are attempts to take something tangled and make it legible, first to myself and then to others.

When I write code, I'm forced to be precise about what I actually mean. The compiler is an unsentimental reader. When I write essays, I get the same gift in a slower form: the sentence that won't quite resolve is usually a thought that isn't finished.

So I keep this notebook. Some of it is in English, some in Chinese, depending on which language the thought was born in. None of it is final. That's rather the point: these are notes toward understanding, not conclusions.
